# AMR Genomics Analysis

## Objective
To detect and interpret antimicrobial resistance (AMR) genes in bacterial genomes for public health surveillance.

---

## Workflow

1. Quality control of sequencing data  
2. Genome analysis using bioinformatics pipelines  
3. Detection of AMR genes  
4. Interpretation of resistance profiles  

---

## Tools Used

- Bactopia  
- Kraken2  
- AMRFinderPlus  
- Abricate  
- MLST  

---

## Example AMR Genes Identified

| Gene | Resistance | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|----------|
| blaTEM-1 | Beta-lactams | Enzyme degradation |
| tet(A) | Tetracycline | Efflux pump |
| sul2 | Sulfonamides | Target modification |
| qnrS1 | Fluoroquinolones | Protection protein |

---

## Interpretation

The presence of genes such as **blaTEM-1** and **tet(A)** indicates resistance to commonly used antibiotics. This information is important for guiding treatment decisions and monitoring antimicrobial resistance trends.
